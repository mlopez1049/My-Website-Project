Conservation of Great White Sharks

About the Great White Shark

One of the world's largest predators is the Great White shark. The aerodynamic shape of the

shark enables it to swim efficiently for extended lengths of time. When necessary, the shark can

produce rapid bursts of speed to capture prey or breach the surface. The Great White is found

around the world in almost every ocean.

Why Conservation is Crucial for Great White Sharks

The world's environment and ecosystem is a delicate, natural balance. As the world's largest predator,

the Great White Shark plays an important role in maintaining the marine life balance.

As an example, the Great White's control the world's seal population.

How You Can Help Great White Sharks

One of the most important ways to help is by supporting research. Research involves tagging, and

learning more about the elusive creatures. Understanding more about the animals helps to raise awareness

and share facts about the animals.
